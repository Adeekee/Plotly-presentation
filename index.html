Key Takeaways

What the data reveals

01 — Orbital Mechanics

Kepler's Third Law holds with extraordinary precision. The log-log slope of 1.5 was discovered in 1619 — long before anyone knew why.

02 — Temperature Gradient

Temperature follows an inverse power law with distance. Neptune sits at −201°C — colder than dry ice. Yet proximity isn't everything: Venus is hotter than Mercury.

03 — Size Discontinuity

The gas giants are not just larger — they represent a categorical break. Jupiter's radius is 11× Earth's; its mass is 318× greater.

04 — Plotly's Power

Interactive visualisation reveals structure impossible to see in static charts. Hover, zoom, and filter to find your own patterns in the data.
